Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The most widespread epidemic of Ebola virus disease (commonly known as "Ebola") in history began in Guinea on December 2013 and continued with significant loss of life for over two years until being declared over in January 2016, though the World Health Organization warns that small outbreaks may continue to be reported.[11] The epidemic was centred in the West African nations of Liberia, Guinea and Sierra Leone, with minor outbreaks elsewhere. It has caused significant mortality, with reported case fatality rates of up to 70%[12][13][14][note 1] and specifically 57â€“59% among hospitalized patients.[15] Ebola virus disease was first described in 1976 in two simultaneous outbreaks in South Sudan and Democratic Republic of the Congo; this is the first Ebola outbreak to occur in the West African subcontinent. The outbreak began in Guinea in December 2013 and then spread to Liberia and Sierra Leone.[12] Small outbreaks occurred in Nigeria and Mali,[6][16] and isolated cases occurred in Senegal,[17] the United Kingdom and Sardinia.[14] Imported cases in the United States and Spain led to secondary infections of medical workers but did not spread further.[18][19] As of 14 January 2016, the World Health Organization (WHO) and respective governments have reported a total of 28,638 suspected cases and 11,315 deaths,[2] though the WHO believes that this substantially understates the magnitude of the outbreak.[20][21] The WHO has warned that further small outbreaks of the disease may occur in the future, and vigilance should be maintained.[22]
